# 2017A_FE-A_68

The RFP is presented to each vendor that has responded to an RFI. Which of the following should be done beforehand to proceed fairly with the future vendor selection processes?

a) Besides the items of an RFP, giving a high evaluation to a vendor who is familiar with the current system to select that vendor easily
b) Establishing processes of selection such as setting evaluation standards or weighting rules of conformity to the request items.
c) Issuing unofficial agreements to all vendors to whom the RFP has been presented and requesting them to prepare a contract and a statement of work to accelerate the conclusion of the contract after the vendor selection.
d) Preparing a bailout for a vendor who received a high evaluation on the response to the RFI, in case it fails in the selection.
?
b) Establishing processes of selection such as setting evaluation standards or weighting rules of conformity to the request items.
### Explanation

To ensure **fairness and objectivity**, evaluation criteria and weighting rules must be **established in advance** — before reviewing proposals — so all vendors are assessed on the same transparent basis.

The other options compromise fairness:
- **A** → preferential treatment based on familiarity is biased
- **C** → issuing unofficial agreements before selection creates legal risk and is premature
- **D** → a bailout for a favored vendor undermines the integrity of the selection process
