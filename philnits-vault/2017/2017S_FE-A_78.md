# 2017S_FE-A_78
When the FIFO method is applied to the purchase and selling record shown below, what is the inventory value at the end of March?

| Date | Transaction | Units | Price |
|------|-------------|-------|-------|
| Mar 1 | Stock | 400 | 0.40 |
| Mar 4 | Purchase | 600 | 0.42 |
| Mar 8 | Sold | 500 | — |
| Mar 10 | Purchase | 500 | 0.43 |
| Mar 15 | Purchase | 800 | 0.42 |
| Mar 20 | Sold | 1000 | — |
| Mar 24 | Purchase | 200 | 0.44 |
| Mar 30 | Sold | 500 | — |

a) 214
b) 241
c) 300
d) 880
?
a) 214
### Explanation

**FIFO**: oldest inventory is sold first.

Total purchased: 400+600+500+800+200 = 2,500 units. Total sold: 500+1,000+500 = 2,000 units. **Remaining: 500 units**.

Tracing FIFO (oldest first): 400@0.40, 600@0.42, 500@0.43, then 500 of 800@0.42 are sold. Remaining inventory:
- 300 units @ 0.42 + 200 units @ 0.44
- $= (300 \times 0.42) + (200 \times 0.44) = 126 + 88 = \mathbf{\$214}$
