# 2017S_FE-A_40
A man-in-the-middle attack happens when an attacker intercepts communication and spoofs the identities of either of the two parties involved. How can such an attack be effectively countered?

a) Such an attack can be countered by a cyclic redundancy check (CRC).
b) Such an attack can be countered by having an authentication through a digital signature before accepting someone's public key.
c) Such an attack can be countered by using a parity bit.
d) Such an attack can be countered by using hashing algorithms.
?
b) Such an attack can be countered by having an authentication through a digital signature before accepting someone's public key.
### Explanation

A **MITM attack** often works by substituting the attacker's public key during key exchange. Using **digital signatures** to authenticate public keys (e.g., via a Certificate Authority / PKI) ensures the key genuinely belongs to the claimed party.

- **CRC** and **parity bits**: detect transmission errors, not identity fraud
- **Hashing**: ensures data integrity but not authentication of identity
