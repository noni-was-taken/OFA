# 2017A_FE-A_16

Below is the list of processes, P1, P2, P3, and P4, and their burst time for CPU scheduling algorithms. [Table: Process P1 Burst time 9 | P2 Burst time 5 | P3 Burst time 7 | P4 Burst time 4] Which of the following combinations is the average waiting time in millisecond for a First-Come, First-Serve (FCFS) scheduling and Shortest-Job-First (SJF) scheduling given that the process arrives in the order P1, P2, P3, and P4, and the latency can be ignored.

a) [Average waiting time under FCFS: 9.5, SJF: 6.25]
b) [Average waiting time under FCFS: 9.0, SJF: 7.0]
c) [Average waiting time under FCFS: 11.0, SJF: 7.25]
d) [Average waiting time under FCFS: 11.5, SJF: 8.0]
?
c) [Average waiting time under FCFS: 11.0, SJF: 7.25]
### Explanation

**FCFS — processes run in arrival order: P1, P2, P3, P4**

| Process | Burst | Wait |
|---------|-------|------|
| P1 | 9 | 0 |
| P2 | 5 | 9 |
| P3 | 7 | 14 |
| P4 | 4 | 21 |

$$\text{Avg wait (FCFS)} = \frac{0 + 9 + 14 + 21}{4} = \frac{44}{4} = 11.0 \text{ ms}$$

**SJF — shortest job first: P4 (4), P2 (5), P3 (7), P1 (9)**

| Process | Burst | Wait |
|---------|-------|------|
| P4 | 4 | 0 |
| P2 | 5 | 4 |
| P3 | 7 | 9 |
| P1 | 9 | 16 |

$$\text{Avg wait (SJF)} = \frac{0 + 4 + 9 + 16}{4} = \frac{29}{4} = 7.25 \text{ ms}$$
