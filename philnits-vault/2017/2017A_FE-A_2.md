# 2017A_FE-A_2

When two unbiased coins are tossed and an unbiased six-sided die is rolled at the same time, what is the probability that at least one coin shows heads and the die shows an even number?

a) 1/4
b) 3/8
c) 1/2
d) 3/4
?
b) 3/8
### Explanation

**Step 1: P(at least one head)**

Using the complement — P(no heads) = both tails:

$$P(\text{no heads}) = \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{4}$$

$$P(\text{at least one head}) = 1 - \frac{1}{4} = \frac{3}{4}$$

**Step 2: P(die shows even)**

Even numbers on a die: {2, 4, 6} → 3 out of 6:

$$P(\text{even}) = \frac{1}{2}$$

**Step 3: Combine (events are independent)**

$$P = \frac{3}{4} \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{3}{8}$$
