# 2017S_FE-A_1
For an 8-bit binary number, which of the following sets the middle 4 bits to 1s while inverting the remaining bits?

a) Performing a bitwise AND operation with 00111100
b) Performing a bitwise NAND operation with 11000011
c) Performing a bitwise OR operation with 11000011
d) Performing a bitwise XOR operation with 00111100
?
b) Performing a bitwise NAND operation with 11000011
### Explanation

We need two effects at once: set the **middle 4 bits** (positions 2–5) to 1, and **invert** the outer 4 bits (positions 0–1 and 6–7).

A **NAND** of two bits produces `NOT(A AND B)`. Using the mask `11000011`:

- For the **outer bits** (mask = 1): `NOT(bit AND 1)` = `NOT(bit)` → **inverts** the outer bits ✅
- For the **middle bits** (mask = 0): `NOT(bit AND 0)` = `NOT(0)` = **1** → forces middle bits to 1 ✅

No other single operation achieves both effects simultaneously with a single mask.
