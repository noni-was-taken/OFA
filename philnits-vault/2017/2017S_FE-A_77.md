# 2017S_FE-A_77
Company A has two media plans. One plan is for a series of 30-second radio advertisements (ads) and the other is for 60-second TV ads. The company is considering an exposure of at least 60 points in the city and at most 40 points outside the city.

| | Cost | In-city exposure | Outside-city exposure |
|-|------|-----------------|----------------------|
| TV | 2000 | 8 | 6 |
| Radio | 800 | 10 | 3 |

Let x = number of TV ads, y = number of radio ads. Which set of expressions minimizes total cost?

a) $8x+6y \ge 60$, $10x+3y \le 40$, $x,y \ge 0$; minimize $2000x+800y$
b) $8x+10y \ge 60$, $6x+3y \le 40$, $x,y \ge 0$; minimize $2000x+800y$
c) $8x+6y \le 60$, $10x+3y \le 40$, $x,y \ge 0$; minimize $2000x+800y$
d) $8x+10y \le 60$, $6x+3y \le 40$, $x,y \ge 0$; minimize $2000x+800y$
?
b) $8x+10y \ge 60$, $6x+3y \le 40$, $x,y \ge 0$; minimize $2000x+800y$
### Explanation

Let **x = TV ads**, **y = radio ads**.

**In-city exposure** (≥ 60): $8x + 10y \ge 60$ (TV=8 pts, Radio=10 pts in-city) ✅

**Outside-city exposure** (≤ 40): $6x + 3y \le 40$ (TV=6 pts, Radio=3 pts outside) ✅

**Objective**: minimize $2000x + 800y$

This matches option **b**.
